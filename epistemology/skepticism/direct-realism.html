Moore's proof of an external world

The argument

  1. Here is a hand, and here is another.
  2. Therefore, two human hands exist.

Conditions on a rigorous proof

  1. The premises are different from the conclusion.
  2. The premises are known to be true.
  3. The conclusion follows from the premises.

Knowledge and proof

Moore admits that he does not have a proof of his premise, but he says this is not what he needs. All that he needs (by condition 2) is that the premise be known to be true, and it obviously is.

Thus, Moore is relying on the idea that there are things we can know without proof. Instead, we simply know them directly. We can call this position direct realism.
