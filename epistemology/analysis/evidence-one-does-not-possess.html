Case 1

In front of the library detective, Tom takes a library book from the shelf and hides it in his coat. He then walks out of the library without renting it and brazenly flaunts it outside. The library detective sees all of this and reports it to the police. The library detective is confident that it is Tom who stole the book.

Later, Tom's mother testifies that Tom has a twin brother who has a naughty streak, and Tom could not have taken the book because he is actually thousands of miles away. The library detective does not hear this testimony. Tom's mother is lying, and it was Tom who took the book.

Does the library detective know that Tom took the book?

Case 2

Donald has gone to Italy. Fred saw him off at the airport, and he told Fred he would be there for the next three months. For some reason, Donald now wants to trick Fred into thinking he is in California. So he sends letters to a friend there and has her mail them to Fred as if she is Donald. But Fred have not received the mail, and he continue to believe that Donald is in Italy, which is true.

Does Fred know that Donald is in Italy?

Case 3

A political leader is assassinated, but his associates attempt to cover it up by releasing a report that the bullet actually hit someone else. They announce this false report on national TV and most everyone catches it. But there was a reporter at the scene who witnessed the assassination. She gets the real story into her newspaper, which has a rather low circulation.

Jill happens to buy the paper and reads the real report, but she doesn't turn the TV on for the fake one. On the basis of the newspaper article, Jill comes to believe that the political leader was assassinated, which is true.

Does Jill know that the political leader was assassinated?*
