Subjective v. objective

Imagine that you are thirsty. In front of you is a glass that is full of a clear liquid. The liquid appears to be water. I know (but you don’t) that the liquid is gasoline.

<2->In this scenario, both A and B can be true

  1. A: There is reason for you to drink the liquid in the glass.

  2. B: There is reason for you to not drink the liquid in the glass.

Subjective v. objective

A references a subjective reason Given the state of your knowledge and your desires, it makes sense for you to drink the liquid.

<2->B references an objective reason Given the state of the world as it actually is and your desires, it doesn’t make sense for you to drink the liquid.

Our goal as inquirers is to come to mutually accept objective reasons. But if someone’s behavior doesn’t make sense from our perspective, it can be valuable to see if we can make sense of it in terms of the subjective reasons they have at their disposal.
