Reason as philosophical method

Last class, we suggested that the value of philosophy comes in providing depth of understanding

One's understanding is shallow when they merely believe something without reflection or consideration of why they believe it.

One's understanding becomes deeper when they acquire some reason for holding their belief.

It is even deeper when they can share that reason with others.

An important element of philosophical method involves the giving and receiving reasons for belief.

The word reason can be used in many different ways

To help get a grip on what it means to engage in the practice of giving and receiving reasons for beliefs, let's examine some of the different kinds of reasons we might profer for a position.
