Basic structure of arguments

A philosophical argument is...

A set of propositions

Propositions are statements that make some claim about the world.

One of these is labeled the conclusion.

The rest are called the premises.

And a relation among them

The premises are said to support the conclusion.

We can also say that the conclusion is believed as an inference from the premises.

In order to provide a complete reason in favor of your conclusion, you need to provide both the supporting premises and the nature of the support those premises provide for the conclusion. When you’ve done that, you’ve given your reasoning in favor of the conclusion.
