Working through an argument

Authors don't always lay their argument out in a neat fashion for you. You may have to do some work in bringing the argument out.

First, try to determine the author's primary conclusion.

Then look for major premises that the author explicitly mentions in favor of the conclusion.

But don't assume that the author will explicitly say everything that is important to the argument. They may leave some premises implicit. It is important to look for these and add them to your argument structure.

Once you have all the premises in place, make sure you understand the reasoning involved in getting from the premises to the conclusion.

Then, you can start to evaluate the argument.

  • Critique the reasoning: validity/strength
  • Refute a premise: soundness/cogency
