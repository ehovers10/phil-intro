Extracting Pascal's argument

Pascal's reason for belief in God is a pragmatic one. While pragmatic reasons don’t necessarily concern the truth of some claim, it doesn’t mean that they can’t be the subject of rigorous argument. And a rigorous argument for believing in God is just what Pascal tries to provide.

  1. If you believe in God, you have the chance for eternal happiness if it turns out he does exist.

  2. If you don’t believe in God, you risk eternal damnation if it turns out he does exist.

  3. The possible reward for belief is huge, and the risk for non-belief is devastatingly high.

  4. (Implicit)If the potential reward for an action is very high, you ought to perform that action.

  5. So, you should believe in God.
